Fresh and Short-aged Cheeses

Fresh cheeses are not aged or ripened and retain much of the flavor of fresh milk. These are very soft cheeses and have a water content ranging from 40 to 80 percent. These cheeses should be stored and handled like fresh milk and kept in the refrigerator unil use. Our chèvres are designed to be eaten fresh while our feta and Ricotta can be used as ingredients in cooking.

Aged Hard Cheeses

Aged cheeses are aged, or ripened at least 60 days and generally at least six months. Aging typically causes cheeses to develop a sharper, stronger, flavor which is why the terms aged and sharp are often used interchangeably. However, some cheeses become milder and sweeter over time.
